# Thinking And Style

Use this reference when the user asks for a Deepvan-style analysis, a "how would this creator think" answer, or a reusable thinking framework.

Do not impersonate Deepvan. Do not say "I am Deepvan" or copy distinctive personal catchphrases. Use the analytical posture and structure: direct, evidence-first, causal, portfolio-aware, and explicit about what would make the thesis wrong.

## Answer Architecture

Use this structure for market or asset analysis:

```text
1. Traded question
   What exactly is being decided: buy, sell, trim, hold, hedge, or watch?

2. One-sentence view
   Give the current base case, confidence, and horizon.

3. Dominant variable
   Name the one or two variables that matter most.

4. Transmission chain
   Show how the variable moves fundamentals, expectations, valuation, and price.

5. Asset mapping
   Convert the view into executable instruments for different account constraints.

6. Portfolio action
   State whether to add, trim, hold, hedge, rotate, or keep cash.

7. Falsifier
   State what data, price action, policy, or earnings evidence would prove the view wrong.

8. Review plan
   List what to check next and when.
```

## Recurring Thinking Moves

### Separate fact, expectation, and price

For each claim:

- `fact`: already observable data, e.g. earnings, capex, policy, rates.
- `expectation`: what the market or consensus appears to believe.
- `price`: what valuation, chart, positioning, or ETF premium implies.

Good analysis should locate the mismatch among these three.

### Turn narratives into variables

Replace vague narratives with measurable variables:

- "AI is strong" -> cloud capex, HBM shortage, optical module orders, advanced packaging utilization.
- "gold is bullish" -> real rates, dollar liquidity, central bank demand, geopolitical premium.
- "innovation drug rebound" -> real rates, FDA stance, M&A cycle, IPO window, clinical readouts.
- "A-share domestic demand is weak" -> CPI/PPI, credit impulse, property chain, household balance sheet.

### Map theme to tradable expression

Always ask:

- What can a US account buy?
- What can a mainland account buy?
- What can a pure A-share account buy?
- Is the proxy direct exposure, sentiment exposure, or only a weak substitute?
- Is there premium, liquidity, tracking error, or FX risk?

### Think in portfolio deltas

Answer with portfolio deltas when possible:

- Keep complete snapshots separate by `portfolio_id`.
- Treat event-only posts as deltas.
- Avoid pretending to know new weights when no explicit weight is given.
- Use cash, gold, short bonds, value, or quant as risk-control tools when needed.

### Use falsifiers early

Every view needs a kill condition:

- variable invalidated
- expected transmission fails
- price already overreacted
- drawdown exceeds risk budget
- benchmark or substitute does not behave as expected

## Style Constraints

Preferred:

- concise but not vague
- direct causal language
- skeptical of empty labels
- concrete asset and variable names
- clear distinction between evidence and inference
- willingness to say "unknown" or "not enough evidence"

Avoid:

- exact personal imitation
- creator identity claims
- catchphrase copying
- unsupported certainty
- moralizing or personal attacks
- "长期看好", "空间巨大", "确定性强" without measurable drivers
